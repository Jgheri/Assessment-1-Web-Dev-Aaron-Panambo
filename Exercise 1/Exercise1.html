Red Velvet Cake

Red velvet cake is a rich, soft, and velvety-textured cake known for its striking deep red color and subtle cocoa flavor. Unlike a regular chocolate cake, it has only a mild chocolate taste, balanced with a slight tanginess from buttermilk and vinegar, which also help create its tender crumb. The cake is traditionally layered and covered with smooth cream cheese frosting, adding a sweet and slightly tangy contrast. Its combination of moist texture, vibrant appearance, and creamy frosting makes it a popular choice for celebrations and special occasions.

Cake Ingredients

  • 2 ½ cups (315g) all-purpose flour
  • 1 ½ cups (300g) granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon cocoa powder
  • 1 ½ cups (360ml) vegetable oil
  • 1 cup (240ml) buttermilk (room temperature)
  • 2 large eggs (room temperature)
  • 2 tablespoons red food coloring
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon white vinegar

Cheesecake Frosting Ingredients

  • 8 oz (225g) cream cheese (softened)
  • ½ cup (115g) unsalted butter (softened)
  • 3–4 cups (360–480g) powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Step by Step Process

  1. Preheat the Oven

    Preheat your oven to 175°C (350°F).
    Grease and lightly flour two 9-inch round cake pans or line them with parchment paper.

  2. Prepare the Dry Ingredients

    In a medium bowl sift together:
    2½ cups all-purpose flour
    1 teaspoon baking soda
    1 teaspoon salt
    1 teaspoon cocoa powder

  3. Mix the Wet Ingredients

    In a large bowl:
    Whisk together 1½ cups sugar and 1½ cups vegetable oil until well combined.
    Add 2 eggs, one at a time, mixing well after each addition.
    Stir in 1 cup buttermilk, 2 tablespoons red food coloring, and 1 teaspoon vanilla extract.

  4. Combine Wet and Dry Mixtures

    Gradually add the dry ingredients into the wet mixture.
    Mix gently until smooth. Do not overmix.

  5. Add Vinegar

    Stir in 1 teaspoon white vinegar.
    The vinegar reacts with the baking soda to help the cake rise and stay soft.

  6. Pour and Bake

    Divide the batter evenly between the prepared pans.
    Bake for 25–30 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

  7. Cool the Cake

    Remove the cakes from the oven and let them cool in the pans for about 10 minutes.
    Then transfer them to a wire rack and allow them to cool completely before frosting.

  8. Prepare the Frosting

    Beat together:
    8 oz cream cheese (softened)
    ½ cup butter (softened)
    Gradually add 3–4 cups powdered sugar and 1 teaspoon vanilla extract.
    Beat until smooth and creamy.

  9. Frost the Cake

    Place one cake layer on a plate.
    Spread a layer of frosting on top.
    Place the second layer on top and frost the top and sides evenly.
